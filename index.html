SCREEN PRINT

A guide to understanding the unique art of screenprinting.

What is Screenprinting?

/ˈskrēn ˌprint/

Screen printing by definition is a printing technique whereby a mesh is used to transfer ink onto a substrate, except in areas made impermeable to the ink by a blocking stencil.

Materials

Here are the basic supplies you need to screenprint.

A 150 mesh screen

emulsion

spatula

squeegee

ink

stencil materials

item to print on

cleaning supplies (sponge, waterbucket)

Part One:

Planning

Warning: Please make sure to do the following steps in a dark room!

You want to coat both sides of your silk screen with emulsion (Speedball photo emulsion is a trusted brand).

Use a squeegee or a scoop coater to drag the emulsion onto the screen, making sure the layer is even.

Use a spatula to remove excess emulsion from the screen, and let it set for 30 min.
